THE ORIGIN STORY

Why I built
CyberWatch Kenya

Samuel Adikah — Founder
Nairobi, Kenya 🇰🇪

Kenya is one of the most digitally connected countries in Africa. We were among the first to embrace mobile money, we have one of the highest smartphone adoption rates on the continent, and our young population lives online — banking, shopping, working, connecting.

But with that connectivity came a wave of cybercrime that has cost Kenyans billions of shillings. And the worst part? Most of it was completely avoidable.

I started CyberWatch Kenya because I watched people around me — smart, hardworking Kenyans — lose their savings to scams they never saw coming. A fake job offer here. A phishing link there. An M-PESA "agent" who was actually a fraudster. The scams were everywhere, but the warnings were nowhere.

There was no single place a Kenyan could go to find out what scams were active right now. No early warning system. No community sharing real intelligence. Just people finding out after they'd already been robbed.

So I built one. CyberWatch Kenya monitors the digital landscape for threats targeting Kenyans — on WhatsApp, Facebook, email, phone calls, fake websites — and sends real-time alerts straight to your inbox and phone before these scams can reach you.

Our mission is simple: every Kenyan deserves to know what threats are out there. Every family deserves to keep their money. Every person should be able to use the internet without fear.

CyberWatch Kenya is not just a newsletter. It's a community of Kenyans looking out for each other.

We're just getting started. But with every alert we publish, with every subscriber who shares our warnings in their WhatsApp group, with every scam we expose — we're making Kenya's internet a little bit safer.

Join us. Let's protect each other. 🛡️
